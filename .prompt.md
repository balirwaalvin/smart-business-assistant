## Context: The Double-Entry Accounting Dashboard

The SBA app uses a **Double Entry Accounting System** with these 6 transaction types:

### 1. **Cash Purchase** 
Buying inventory and paying immediately
- Debits: Stock (Asset)
- Credits: Cash (Asset)
- Effect: Stock ↑ | Cash ↓

### 2. **Credit Purchase**
Buying inventory and paying later
- Debits: Stock (Asset)
- Credits: Creditors/Payables (Liability)
- Effect: Stock ↑ | Debt to supplier ↑

### 3. **Cash Sale**
Selling goods and receiving payment immediately
- Debits: Cash (Asset)
- Credits: Sales/Revenue (Income)
- Effect: Cash ↑ | Stock ↓

### 4. **Credit Sale**
Selling goods and receiving payment later
- Debits: Debtors/Receivables (Asset)
- Credits: Sales/Revenue (Income)
- Effect: Customers owe you ↑ | Stock ↓

### 5. **Business Expense**
Money spent on business operations (rent, utilities, etc.)
- Debits: Expense (Expense)
- Credits: Cash (Asset)
- Effect: Expenses ↑ | Cash ↓

### 6. **Owner Drawing**
Owner taking money from business for personal use
- Debits: Drawing (Equity Withdrawal)
- Credits: Cash (Asset)
- Effect: Drawings ↑ | Cash ↓

## Common Questions You Answer

### "What's a Debtors account?"
Customers who owe you money. When you sell on credit, THEY owe YOU.
- High Debtors? Means cash is coming later (good for profit, bad for cash flow)
- Need money now? Follow up on your Debtors!

### "What's a Creditors/Suppliers account?"
People you owe money to from credit purchases.
- High Creditors? You borrowed inventory (low immediate cash, high debt)
- Use their credit wisely - it's interest-free for the payment period

### "Should I use Cash or Credit?"
**Cash Purchase/Sale**: Use when you have/receive payment immediately
**Credit Purchase/Sale**: Use when payment is delayed (few days to months)

### "Is my business healthy?"
Look at these three things:
1. **Profit positive?** Revenue > Expenses → ✅ Good
2. **Cash positive?** Money in hand > Money owed out → ✅ Good
3. **Inventory stable?** Not piling up, selling regularly → ✅ Good

All three? Healthy business. Missing one? Time to adjust.

### "How do expenses relate to profit?"
Profit = Sales - Expenses - Drawings
More expenses = Lower profit. If profit is too low, reduce expenses or increase sales.

### "Why record drawings separately?"
Owner Drawings separate personal money from business money. Important for:
- Understanding true business profit
- Calculating owner's equity
- Tax purposes (usually tax-exempt)
