## App Overview for Users

### What This App Does
The Smart Business Assistant is a **double-entry accounting dashboard** that helps small business owners track:
- **Purchases**: What you're buying for your business
- **Sales**: What you're selling to customers
- **Expenses**: Money spent on business operations
- **Drawings**: Money taken out by business owner
- **Assets**: Cash, inventory, and what customers owe you
- **Liabilities**: What you owe to suppliers

### The Three Core Columns

#### 📦 LEFT COLUMN: Inventory & Suppliers
- **Total Purchases**: Money spent acquiring inventory
- **Suppliers**: Your creditors (people you owe money to)
- Shows the connection: Buying stock increases inventory, decreases cash

#### 💰 CENTER COLUMN: Business Assets & Profit
- **Cash Balance**: Money available in your business
- **Stock (Inventory)**: Physical goods ready to sell
- **Profit/Loss**: How much money your business has earned or lost
- Shows your business's financial heartbeat

#### 🎯 RIGHT COLUMN: Sales & Customers
- **Total Sales**: Revenue from selling to customers
- **Debtors (Customers)**: People who owe you money
- Shows the connection: Selling on credit means cash comes later

## Transaction Entry Guide

### Recording a Purchase (Buying Inventory)

**When to Use**: When you buy physical goods from a supplier

**Access**: Click **"Total Purchases"** card → Select purchase type

#### Choice 1: Cash Purchase
- **You have**: Money now, goods now
- **Accounting**: Stock increases ↑ | Cash decreases ↓
- **Form fields**:
  - **Item**: What you're buying (e.g., "Red Shirts - Size L")
  - **Quantity**: How many units (e.g., "50")
  - **Amount**: Total price paid (e.g., "5000")
  - **Supplier Name**: Who you're buying from (e.g., "Textiles Ltd.")
- **Example**: Buy 50 red shirts for 5000 cash from Textiles Ltd.

#### Choice 2: Credit Purchase
- **You have**: Goods now, pay money later
- **Accounting**: Stock increases ↑ | Debt to supplier increases ↑
- **Form fields**: Same as cash purchase
- **Example**: Buy 50 red shirts for 5000 on credit from Textiles Ltd. (pay in 30 days)

**What Happens After**:
- Dashboard updates immediately
- "Total Purchases" number increases
- "Suppliers" shows updated debt amount
- "Stock" increases regardless of payment method
- "Cash" only decreases for cash purchases

---

### Recording a Sale (Selling to Customers)

**When to Use**: When you sell goods to a customer

**Access**: Click **"Total Sales"** card → Select sale type

#### Choice 1: Cash Sale
- **You have**: Money now, goods gone now
- **Accounting**: Cash increases ↑ | Stock decreases ↓
- **Form fields**:
  - **Item**: What you're selling (e.g., "Red Shirts - Size L")
  - **Quantity**: How many units sold (e.g., "10")
  - **Amount**: Total price received (e.g., "2000")
  - **Customer Name**: Who you're selling to
- **Example**: Sell 10 red shirts for 2000 cash to John's Shop

#### Choice 2: Credit Sale
- **You have**: Goods gone, money comes later
- **Accounting**: Debt from customer increases ↑ | Sale revenue increases ↑
- **Form fields**: Same as cash sale
- **Example**: Sell 10 red shirts for 2000 to John's Shop (they pay in 15 days)

**What Happens After**:
- Dashboard updates immediately
- "Total Sales" number increases
- "Debtors" shows updated money owed to you
- "Stock" decreases regardless of payment method
- "Cash" only increases for cash sales

---

### Recording Expenses (Money Spent on Operations)

**When to Use**: When you spend money on business operations (rent, electricity, advertising, etc.)

**Access**: Click **"Expenses (Drawings)"** card → Select "Business Expense"

**Form fields**:
- **Description**: What the expense is for (e.g., "Office rent - July")
- **Amount**: How much you spent (e.g., "10000")

**Accounting Impact**: Expense increases ↑ | Cash decreases ↓

**Examples**:
- Paid 10000 for office rent
- Paid 2000 for electricity bill
- Paid 500 for advertising on social media
- Paid 1500 for vehicle maintenance

**What Happens After**:
- "Expenses (Drawings)" card increases
- "Cash Balance" decreases
- Your profit decreases (expenses reduce profit)

---

### Recording Drawings (Owner Taking Money Out)

**When to Use**: When you (the owner) take money from the business for personal use

**Access**: Click **"Expenses (Drawings)"** card → Select "Owner Drawing"

**Form fields**:
- **Description**: Purpose or date (e.g., "July personal withdrawal" or "Salary")
- **Amount**: How much you're taking (e.g., "5000")

**Accounting Impact**: Drawing increases ↑ | Cash decreases ↓

**Why Track This**:
- Separate business money from personal money
- Shows how much owner has taken from business
- Important for business taxes and profit calculation

**Examples**:
- Take 5000 as salary
- Take 2000 for personal expenses
- Take 1000 for vacation

**What Happens After**:
- "Expenses (Drawings)" card increases (separate from business expenses)
- "Cash Balance" decreases
- Shows owner impact on business finances

## Understanding Dashboard Numbers

### Left Column (Inventory & Suppliers)
- **Total Purchases**: Sum of all inventory purchases (cash + credit)
- **Suppliers**: Total money owed to suppliers from credit purchases
- These connect: Credit purchases increase both numbers

### Center Column (Business Assets)
- **Cash Balance**: Money available to spend or invest
- **Stock**: Current value of inventory you have
- **Profit/Loss**: Positive = business made money | Negative = business lost money
- **Formula**: Profit = Sales - Expenses - Drawings (simplified)

### Right Column (Sales & Customers)
- **Total Sales**: Sum of all sales (cash + credit)
- **Debtors**: Total money customers owe you from credit sales
- These connect: Credit sales increase both numbers

### Account Relationships Section (Bottom)
Shows the **accounting flow** for each transaction type:
- **Purchases**: Stock ↑ | Cash/Creditors ↓ or ↑
- **Sales**: Cash/Debtors ↑ | Stock ↓
- **Expenses**: Expense ↑ | Cash ↓
- **Drawings**: Drawing ↑ | Cash ↓

## Best Practices & Tips for Users

### 📋 Daily Tasks
1. **Record cash sales immediately** as they happen
2. **Log expenses daily** to avoid forgetting
3. **End of week**: Reconcile with your actual bank balance

### 📅 Weekly Tasks
1. **Review "Total Purchases"** vs actual supplier invoices
2. **Check "Debtors"** column and follow up on unpaid invoices
3. **Verify "Stock"** matches physical inventory if possible

### 🎯 Monthly Tasks
1. **Reconcile all accounts** with bank statements, invoices, and receipts
2. **Review Profit/Loss** to understand business performance
3. **Pay supplier invoices** before due dates (using "Suppliers" for reference)
4. **Collect from debtors** using "Debtors" to track who owes you

### ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Recording same transaction twice** (cash + credit by mistake)
- **Forgetting to record purchases** on credit (increases debt)
- **Not tracking cash sales** (business thinks it made no money)
- **Mixing personal and business money** (use Drawings account)
- **Inventory discrepancies** (record all purchases and sales)

## Accounting Concepts Explained Simply

### Double Entry System (Why It Works)
Every transaction affects **two accounts**:
1. **Cash/Inventory/Debtors** (Asset account)
2. **Suppliers/Sales/Expenses** (Liability/Income/Expense account)

**Benefit**: Finance always balances. If something seems wrong, it usually means a transaction is missing.

### DEBIT vs CREDIT (Simple Version)
- **DEBIT**: Increases assets & expenses | Decreases liabilities & sales
- **CREDIT**: Opposite of debit
- **Don't worry about details** - app handles this automatically!

### Why This Matters
Each transaction shows:
- What happens to your **cash** (do you have more or less?)
- What happens to your **debt** (do you owe more or less?)
- What happens to your **assets** (do you have more inventory or less?)

This gives complete **business visibility**.

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

### "My numbers don't match my bank statement"
1. Check cash sales - did you record all of them?
2. Check credit sales - customers may not have paid yet
3. Check expenses - did you record all checks/transfers?
4. Reconcile: Bank balance should equal (Cash + unpaid Debtors - outstanding Supplier debts)

### "I recorded a transaction wrong"
Contact support with:
- What transaction you recorded
- What the correct details should be
- When you recorded it

### "I forgot to record a purchase"
1. Click "Total Purchases"
2. Choose cash or credit
3. Enter the details
4. The dashboard will update automatically

### "How do I delete a transaction?"
Currently, transactions are permanent (for audit purposes). Contact support if you need corrections.

## Tips for Financial Health

### Weekly Health Check
```
Do this: Compare three numbers
- Your actual cash in hand
- Dashboard "Cash Balance"
- Last recorded transaction date

Should match? YES = you're on track!
```

### Monthly Profit Check
```
Formula: Total Sales - Total Expenses - Drawings = Profit/Loss
- Positive = You're making money ✅
- Negative = Spending more than earning ⚠️
- If negative, reduce expenses or increase sales
```

### Cash Flow Tip
```
Cash is different from profit!
- You can be profitable but have low cash (too much credit sales)
- You can have high cash but low profit (buying too much inventory)

Watch BOTH for healthy business.
```

## Stock Workflow (Latest)

### Add Available Stock from Stock Card
- Click the **Stock / Inventory** card in the dashboard center column.
- Enter:
   - **Stock Name** (product name)
   - **Quantity to Add**
   - **Low Stock Threshold** (minimum safe quantity)
- This increases available stock for that specific item.

### Purchase and Sale Product Selection
- Purchase and Sale forms now use a stock dropdown tied to your inventory.
- **Sales** must select an existing stock item.
- **Purchases** can select an existing item or choose **Add new stock item**.

### Automatic Stock Deduction on Sales
- When you record a sale, the sold quantity is deducted from available stock automatically.
- If sale quantity exceeds available stock, the app blocks the transaction and explains the shortage.

### TUNDA AI Low-Stock Reminders
- TUNDA AI shows reminders when item quantity falls to or below your threshold.
- Reminder format: item name, current quantity, and threshold.
- Use these reminders to restock early and avoid stockouts.
